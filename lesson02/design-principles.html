Visual Hierarchy

Amazon

Visual Hierarchy is the arranging of certain elements to show off their importance to the user. In the provided image, we can see that Amazon uses this particular technique with the "Add to cart" and "Buy Now" buttons. These are the main 'call to actions' on the page, which is why they have colors that are easily noticeable and distinguishable by the user.

White Space and Clean Design

BYU-Idaho

White space refers to the sections of a web page that are not occupied by any content on the screen. Just because there is empty space, does not mean that the space is being wasted. For this example, I have chosen the BYU-Idaho home page, as it has white space between sections. This is done to make the page easier to understand, and so that there is a clear distinction between elements on the page.

Fitt's law

Google

Fitt's Law refers to the amount of time required for a user to move a pointer to its target area. For my example, I have chosen Google. It has a very simple interface, and you will not have to move your cursor or finger far to press on the result you are looking for.
