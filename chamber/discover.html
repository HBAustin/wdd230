History of Kloof

Kloof, in KwaZulu-Natal, began as Richmond Farm No. 999, a sprawling 6,000-acre property. Awarded to William Swan Field in 1851 for his service to Natal Colony, it later passed to his son, J.C. Field, who shaped the area's history.

After his passing, subdivisions led to Kloof's emergence as a residential area. Wealthy Durban residents bought plots for summer retreats, drawn by the elevation's relief from city heat. Originally named 'Krantzkloof,' it was later changed to 'Kloof' to avoid railway confusion. The area transformed from grassland to wooded splendor by the 1890s.

Kloof evolved further with permanent residents in the 1960s and 1970s, characterized by spacious houses nestled in lush surroundings, reflecting its tranquil charm and historical lineage.
